Why I Created Baudelaire Clinics

I founded Baudelaire Clinics out of frustration—out of a deep, personal need to fix something I saw repeatedly.

People are lost in the system. They'd see a dermatologist for a rash, a beauty therapist for their skin texture, and an aesthetic practitioner for injectables—all in different places, with different opinions, and no one truly looking at them as a whole.

I kept thinking: Why isn’t there one place where everything comes together—where science and beauty speak the same language?

That question became a calling.

Baudelaire Clinics is my answer—a space where cosmetic dermatology, evidence-based skincare, and aesthetic medicine come together as one. We treat skin as more than surface. It’s biology, identity, and expression.

The name comes from the poet Baudelaire, who believed beauty is imperfect, complex, and deeply human. I agree. I didn’t want a clinic that made everyone look the same. I wanted a clinic that helps you look like you, at your best.

This clinic isn’t about trends or quick fixes. It’s about clarity, trust, and creating tailored care that fits you—not the other way around

Who Am I?

I’ve always seen skin differently—not just as something to treat, but something to understand.

I grew up in Nice, a region that carries a quiet but rich legacy in cosmetics and skin care, where nature and science have always worked side by side.

My journey began in pharmacy, where I learned to understand the body through chemistry—how active ingredients interact with the skin, how it heals, absorbs, and protects. But I wanted to go deeper. That led me to a PhD in photobiology at the University of Bath, where I explored how light shapes skin health, ageing, and inflammation. It opened a new way of seeing skin—not just as something visible, but as something living, dynamic, and responsive at the cellular level.

Still, I didn’t want to stay behind the microscope. I wanted to work with people, not just theories. So, I trained in clinical dermatology at the Royal College of Physicians of Ireland, gaining hands-on expertise in diagnosing and treating skin conditions across all skin types. Finally, in London, I completed advanced training in aesthetic medicine, learning how to enhance what’s already there—subtly, precisely, and with purpose.

Each step built on the last. Science, medicine, and aesthetics merged into one clear idea: to treat the skin not in parts, but as a whole—intelligently, individually, and beautifully.

Baudelaire Clinics brings it all together, a place where the skin is treated not just as something perfect, but something to protect, understand, and celebrate.
